# Uses & for whom

> _Who benefits from CatalogueCanvas, and the concrete jobs it does for them._

## Who it's for

<!-- One short profile per audience. Edit/add to match your real users. -->

### Artists & illustrators
Catalogue a body of work, keep notes and metadata per piece, and publish a clean public
portfolio without building a website.

### Generative & creative coders
Each ZIP can hold the rendered image **plus** the source (`.py`, `.r`, `.js`, `.p5`, `.json`, …),
keeping output and code together as one item.

### Designers
Organize design assets into collections, tag them, and share curated sets privately
or publicly.

### Studios, archives & small teams
A self-hosted, low-footprint catalogue that lives on your own hardware — no SaaS, no per-seat cost.

### Self-hosting enthusiasts
Runs in one Docker container with SQLite; full export/backup built in.

## What you can do with it

| Goal | How CatalogueCanvas helps |
|---|---|
| Keep a private master catalogue of work | Ingest ZIPs, tag, note, organize into collections |
| Pair finished work with its source files | Multiple files per item, viewable or downloadable |
| Auto-generate descriptions | Vision LLM (OpenAI-compatible, local or hosted) |
| Share a curated showcase | Public portfolios at `/p/<slug>` |
| Store work across disks | Multiple libraries |
| Protect your data | One-click database + full-asset backup |

## When it may _not_ be the right fit

<!-- Honesty here builds trust. Adjust to reality. -->

- You're cataloguing a photo library — for that, use a dedicated photo manager like [Immich](https://immich.app/). CatalogueCanvas is built for digital art, illustration, and generative/algorithmic work, not photo collections.
- You need multi-user accounts with granular per-user permissions today (current model: single admin + public portfolio viewers).
- You want a hosted/managed SaaS with no self-hosting.
